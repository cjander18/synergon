Frequently asked questions

What is Synergon?

A free web app for structured group deliberation: a coordinator poses a question, team members answer anonymously to their peers, the coordinator consolidates the pooled answers, and the group refines and ranks them over multiple rounds (the Delphi method / Nominal Group Technique). It runs entirely in the browser — no server, no accounts.

How anonymous is it, exactly?

Anonymous to other participants: responses are pooled with identities stripped before anyone evaluates anything, so no colleague can tell who said what — that is what removes anchoring, seniority bias, and self-censorship. One honest boundary: the coordinator who sends the invitations knows who received each link. We never claim more than that; the full threat model is public.

Where does my data live?

In your own browser (IndexedDB), and nowhere else. The app makes no network requests — no telemetry, no analytics, no accounts. Responses travel between people as end-to-end encrypted envelopes carried by the users themselves (a link, a file, or a QR code), and each envelope's password travels separately, out of band.

Is it really free? What's the catch?

The local product is complete and free, forever — and there's a structural reason to believe that: serving a static page costs us nothing per user. The boundary is principled: anything that runs on your device stays free; paid tiers, when they exist, will only ever add hosted convenience (like links that deliver themselves through a relay that sees only ciphertext). The honest catch today: you carry the envelopes yourself — copy a link out, paste a response back, each round.

What happens if I clear my browser or switch computers?

Local storage is the database, so clearing it removes your workflows — and some browsers (notably Safari) can evict site storage after about a week of inactivity. Use the Export workflow button for a backup file you can re-import anywhere. Exports contain only what's stored: ciphertext, never passwords.

How does the encryption work?

Standard constructions via the browser's built-in Web Crypto API: PBKDF2-SHA256 key derivation from a per-participant password, AES-256-GCM authenticated encryption, with routing headers authenticated so envelopes can't be tampered with or misrouted. No custom primitives, no third-party crypto code. Honest status: it has not been independently audited — the implementation is ~150 lines, open source, and adversarial review is invited.

Does it work offline / on phones / from a file?

Yes. Answering and consolidating need no connectivity — only moving envelopes between people does, and that happens over your own channels. The interface works on phones (contributors usually open links there), and the app can even be run from a local file.

What does this website collect?

The marketing pages (not the app) count visits with a cookie-less, open-source counter. The app itself collects nothing. Details on the privacy page.

How many rounds does a deliberation take?

Typically two or three: collect ideas → consolidate and clarify → rank or score. The built-in demo workflow shows a complete three-round arc in about two minutes of reading.
