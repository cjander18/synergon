Privacy

The short version: the Synergon app collects nothing.

The app

Everything you do in the app — workflows, rounds, responses — is stored in your own browser and nowhere else. The app makes no network requests of its own: no analytics, no telemetry, no error reporting, no accounts. Responses travel between people as end-to-end encrypted envelopes that you carry yourself (a link, a file, or a QR code). The full threat model is public, and so is all of the source code.

One honest boundary, stated everywhere in the product: responses are anonymous to other participants. The coordinator who sends the invitations knows who received each link.

This website

The marketing pages (this page and the landing page — not the app) count visits with GoatCounter, an open-source, cookie-less counter. It records that a page was viewed and which button was clicked, without cookies, fingerprinting, or personal identification. We use it to answer exactly one question: do people who read the landing page go on to open the app?

Email

If you write to synergonlabs@gmail.com, that's normal email: we read it, we may reply, and it stays in the mailbox. We don't add you to any list unless you ask for updates, and you can ask to be removed at any time.

Changes

Any change to what is collected — especially anything touching the app's collects-nothing guarantee — will be recorded in the project's public architecture decision records before it ships.
