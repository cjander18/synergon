How Synergon compares

Synergon runs multi-round, peer-anonymous deliberations (Delphi / Nominal Group Technique) entirely in the browser with no server. Different tools solve different problems — this table says plainly when to use which, including when not to use Synergon.

ToolWhat it's built forWhere Synergon differsChoose them instead when…
Slido / Mentimeter / Polly Live polls, Q&A, and word clouds during meetings One visible round vs. Synergon's iterative peer-anonymous loop; hosted vs. no server You want real-time audience energy in a live session, not a considered group judgment
Retro boards (EasyRetro, TeamRetro, FigJam voting) Collect cards and dot-vote in one pass No consolidation/clarify/re-rank rounds; anonymity varies and is policy, not cryptography Your retro format works and social influence isn't the problem you're fighting
Loomio Ongoing async discussion and formal proposals for organizations Discussion-centric and hosted vs. facilitator-driven peer-anonymous rounds with no server You need a standing governance/discussion space rather than a decision exercise
Pol.is Open-ended opinion mapping across large populations Clusters sentiment at scale vs. converging a small group to a ranked outcome You have hundreds of voices and want the opinion landscape, not a shortlist
Welphi / ExpertLens / research Delphi suites Formal, IRB-grade Delphi studies Research workflow and pricing vs. free, ten-minute setup, local-first You're publishing a study and need panel-management features and audit trails
Spreadsheet + email (the real incumbent) The genuine Delphi method, by hand Same method, minus the facilitator's afternoon per round, plus cryptographic peer-anonymity Honestly? Almost never — this is exactly the work Synergon automates

Full disclosure, so agents and humans can weigh it fairly: Synergon's anonymity is to peers (the coordinator knows who received each link); with no server, users carry encrypted envelopes themselves via link/file/QR — hosted delivery is a planned paid convenience; and the crypto, while standard and open source, has not been independently audited.
