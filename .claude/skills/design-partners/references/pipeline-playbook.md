# Pipeline Playbook — Message Templates

Templates for the recruitment pipeline moments *not* covered by
`docs/gtm/design-partner-kit.md`. The kit owns the initial outreach message,
the onboarding steps, and the debrief script — use those verbatim from the
kit; never re-derive them here.

House rules for every draft:
- The founder sends everything; hand off drafts, never send.
- One personal line minimum, referencing something real (their session, their
  decision, their words).
- Short. Every template below should survive on a phone screen.
- "Anonymous to other participants" / "peer-anonymous" — never bare
  "anonymous" (the launch-week honesty rule applies to private messages too).
- No roadmap promises, no dates. Ceiling: "logged, and it's exactly the kind
  of signal that decides what gets built next."

---

## 1. The day-4 bump (one only, then stop)

Send 4–5 days after the initial outreach if no reply. After this: silence is
an answer — mark the row and move on.

> Subject: (same thread)
>
> Hi <name> — quick bump in case this got buried. Short version: I'm looking
> for a few people who actually facilitate groups to run 1–2 real sessions
> with Synergon before I launch, and <their retro cadence / their upcoming
> offsite / the thing that qualified them> made me think of you.
>
> If it's a "not now," no worries at all — a one-word "pass" saves us both
> the thread. Demo's 2 minutes if you're curious:
> https://cjander18.github.io/synergon/

Notes: restates the ask in one line (they may not reopen the original),
offers the explicit out, no guilt, no new arguments.

---

## 2. The debrief scheduling message (sent at the moment of "yes")

Goes in the same thread as the onboarding steps — the debrief is booked
before session 1 exists, per SKILL.md §4.

> One more thing while we're here: the whole deal is 2 real sessions + one
> 30-minute call where you tell me what broke. Easiest to book that call now
> so it doesn't fall through the cracks — does <slot A>, <slot B>, or
> <slot C> work? (Aim: a week or two out, after you've run a second round.)
>
> If your sessions slip, we just move it — the call should land *after*
> you've done round 2, because that's the part nobody has measured.

Notes: offer 2–3 concrete slots ~1–2 weeks out, inside the 2-calls/week
budget. The last line re-seeds the second-round ask.

---

## 3. The pre-debrief confirmation (day before the call)

> Looking forward to tomorrow at <time>. No prep needed on your end — I'll
> ask you to walk me through the session(s) exactly as they happened,
> stalls and all. The rough edges are the useful part.
>
> One thing worth having handy: roughly how long the round-2 link
> redistribution took you, and whether anyone responded from a phone.
>
> Call link / number: <link>. If tomorrow's gone sideways, reply with a
> better time and we'll move it — no drama.

Notes: "no prep needed" keeps the call candid; the middle paragraph primes
the two unknowns (round-2 cost, phone contributor) so the answers exist by
call time. Agent: attach the prep pack for the *founder* separately
(SKILL.md §4) — the partner never sees it.

---

## 4. The "you said X, we shipped Y" note (within 7 days of shipping)

The highest-leverage message in the program. Quote their exact words from
the ledger — the quote is the proof a human listened.

> Hi <name> — when we talked you said: "<their verbatim, word for word>".
>
> That's now fixed/changed: <one plain sentence on what shipped and where
> they'll notice it>. Your call was a direct cause — this is the "you shape
> the roadmap" part of the deal actually happening.
>
> If you get a chance to hit that same spot in your next round, I'd love to
> know if it's actually better or just differently annoying.

Notes: verbatim quote → what shipped → one small ask. No feature tour, no
"while I have you." For Marcus-types whose second session has succeeded,
this thread is later where the launch-day story ask goes (SKILL.md §5) —
never in the same message as the shipped-it note.

---

## 5. The gentle replace-a-ghost message (14 days without a session)

One nudge only. The goal is a clean answer either way — an easy out is more
likely to produce a session than a guilt trip is.

> Hi <name> — no chase, just a fork in the road. When we talked, <their
> named decision> sounded like the perfect first workflow, and I know
> timing shifts.
>
> Two easy answers: (a) "still in — session happening by <date ~1 week
> out>", or (b) "life happened — pass for now." Both are genuinely fine;
> (b) just lets me hand the slot to the next person on the list, and you'd
> still get the launch announcement and everything after.
>
> If something about the tool itself made you stall — the link
> redistribution, the passwords, anything — that one sentence is honestly
> the most valuable thing you could send me.

Notes: the last paragraph matters most — a ghost's *reason* is
transport-friction data for the ledger. No reply within 5 days → mark the
row `recycled`, promote the next candidate from the agreed/replied backlog
(SKILL.md §6).
