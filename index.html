Synergon runs structured, multi-round deliberations — collect ideas anonymously from peers, consolidate, clarify, then rank — so decisions reflect your group's judgment, not its hierarchy. The method is Delphi; the spreadsheet is gone.

Open Synergon — free Source on GitHub

How it works

  1. Ask. Pose a question and send each teammate a private, encrypted link with its own password. They answer in two minutes — no account, no install.
  2. Consolidate. Responses pool with identities removed. Deduplicate and merge, then send the combined list back for clarification or another pass.
  3. Rank. Everyone scores or ranks privately; Synergon aggregates. You get a shortlist the group actually believes in — with the support to prove it.

Why it's different

A real method, not a poll

Delphi and Nominal Group Technique are decades-proven at removing anchoring, groupthink, and the seniority effect. Synergon runs the full loop, not one round.

Anonymous to peers

Nobody's answer can be traced by colleagues — contributions are pooled with identities stripped before anyone evaluates anything.

Nothing to trust but math

No server. No accounts. Everything runs in your browser; responses travel as end-to-end encrypted envelopes. There is nothing for IT to review.

Free means free

The local product is complete and free, forever. Paid tiers, when they come, only ever add hosted convenience.

An honest note on anonymity

Responses are anonymous to other participants — that is what removes the social bias. The coordinator who sends the invitations knows who received each link. We say this plainly here and inside the product, and the full threat model is public.
